The 9 dual-credit hours mean you pay undergraduate tuition rates for coursework that simultaneously satisfies graduate requirements — one of the most significant financial benefits of the program.
Can I change my mind after enrolling? +
Yes. Students may opt out at any time. If you do, only the undergraduate portion of your coursework will apply toward your undergraduate degree.
Do I graduate with two degrees at once? +
Not simultaneously. You remain classified as an undergraduate until all undergraduate requirements are met. After earning your bachelor’s, you transition to the graduate program and typically finish your master’s within about a year.
What happens to the 6 non-dual-credit hours? +
Up to 6 of the 15 graduate hours taken as an undergrad count only toward the master’s degree. These are applied after you are formally admitted to the EPY graduate program through the MSU Graduate School.
Do I need to apply to the Graduate School separately? +
Yes. You must apply formally to the EPY graduate program through the MSU Graduate School in addition to the accelerated program application.
What are the application deadlines? Free to apply+
Applying to the accelerated program is free. Deadlines are November 1 to begin in the Spring semester, and May 1 to begin in the Fall semester. You will need a 500–750 word statement of purpose.
What if I plan to apply to a different graduate program? +
It depends on how many transfer credits that program allows. Some master’s and graduate programs will accept a number of your completed hours, which could shorten your time to degree. And even if none transfer, having graduate-level coursework on your record makes you a more competitive and distinctive applicant — it signals academic readiness in a way that most applicants cannot.

Important planning notes

  • If you have already completed the undergraduate version of a course (e.g. EPY 4033), you cannot take its graduate version (e.g. EPY 6033) for dual-credit. This is one of the biggest reasons to start the accelerated program early!
  • Scheduling conflicts with your remaining undergraduate requirements may limit which graduate courses you can fit in a given semester.
